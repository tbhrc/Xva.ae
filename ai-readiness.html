Interactive Tool

AI Readiness Check.

A quick self-assessment to help you understand where your organisation stands today across four key dimensions: Strategy, People, Technology and Governance.

Strategy

Vision, value and alignment.

We have a documented business strategy, and AI is discussed in that context rather than as a separate topic.

We can name specific business outcomes we expect from AI, such as faster cycle times or improved customer experience.

There is agreement at leadership level on where AI should be explored first in our organisation.

People

Capability, culture and change capacity.

Key leaders are open to AI and willing to adjust ways of working when there is clear value.

We have staff who understand our processes in detail and can work with consultants on mapping and redesign.

There is a plan or willingness to invest in training so people can use AI tools safely and effectively.

Technology

Systems, data and integration readiness.

We have core systems (for example ERP, CRM, HR) that are reasonably stable and documented.

Operational data required for priority processes is captured in digital systems rather than only in paper or ad hoc spreadsheets.

Our IT team or partners are open to integrating new tools where justified by a clear business case.

Governance

Risk, policy and decision making.

We have clear policies or guidelines on data protection, privacy and acceptable use of AI tools.

There is a defined process for approving or rejecting new AI or automation initiatives.

Senior leaders are willing to experiment with AI in a controlled way rather than waiting for complete certainty.

1 = not true at all, 3 = somewhat true, 5 = very true. You do not need to aim for a perfect score; this is a snapshot to support discussion.
